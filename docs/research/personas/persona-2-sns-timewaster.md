# Persona 2 — "구조를 잃어 시간을 허비하는 사람" (heavy SNS user; research-grounded)

> Part of the LifePlanner customer profile. **Shared foundations (F1–F8), segmentation, POV, and all references
> `[S#]` live in [overview](docs/research/personas/overview.md).** This persona is grounded in behavioral-science +
> market research (not direct interview); the user named/refined it as an expansion target.

**Identity.** Someone with abundant unstructured time whose day goes almost entirely to **social media** —
Instagram, Threads, X, YouTube, TikTok — "communicating with the internet" yet producing nothing. Named/refined
by the user. `[R1-13,17]`

**Snapshot / context.** No external structure (job seeker, freelancer, student on break, remote). Social usage is
already high everywhere — global avg ~2h21m/day, but **Gen Z ~4h/day** and young people ~**6.5h/day online** — and
this persona sits at the top of that curve, with time flowing to **short-form video** (TikTok, Shorts, Reels). `[S18]`
Empty mornings and, long-term, **listlessness/loss of self-worth** ("days lack structure → worthlessness"). `[S8]`

**Why the day leaks (mechanism).**
- **Short-form loops degrade the very faculty needed to act**: heavy short-form use is linked to **worse sustained
  attention and inhibitory control** ("TikTok brain"), and a TikTok interruption was the **"true destroyer of
  memory"** — users forget *what they were about to do* (prospective memory). `[S19]`
- **Passive scrolling deepens the void it fills**: passive use raises **loneliness, FOMO, and social comparison**;
  idealized feeds make one's own life feel unspectacular — so SNS is a **compensatory mechanism** that worsens the
  emptiness. It falsely satisfies Jahoda's **social-contact** function while leaving **time-structure/purpose**
  empty. `[S20][S10]`

**JTBD.** *Functional*: replace aimless scrolling with a **structured day** that yields output. — *Emotional*:
escape the loneliness/FOMO loop; regain purpose and self-respect. `[S10][S20]` — *Social*: real progress instead
of parasocial "communicating with the internet." `[S20]`

**User scenario (설정→발단→행동→결과).** *설정*: 마감 없는 텅 빈 하루. *발단*: 무심코 연 인스타/유튜브/틱톡.
*행동*: 숏폼 변동보상에 빨려들어 몇 시간, 하려던 일도 잊음. *결과*: 저녁, 성과 0 + 외로움·무력감. `[S19][S20]`

**Pains (sharpest first).** ① **Hours vanish into feeds, nothing produced.** ② **Attention/memory eroded** — even
when they mean to act, they forget/can't focus. ③ **Lonelier despite constant "connection"** (FOMO/comparison). `[S19][S20]`

**Motivation.** Beneath the scrolling is an unmet need for **time structure, purpose, and genuine connection** —
SNS is a failed substitute. `[S10][S20]`

**Root cause (5Why).** 시간 허비 → SNS에 빠져서 → 왜 → 구조가 없고 숏폼이 변동보상으로 붙잡아서 → 왜 못 빠져
나오나 → 주의·전망기억이 약해지고 대안 구조가 없어서 → **근본: 자기부여 시간구조의 부재 + 변동보상 환경.** `[S7][S19][S10]`

**Current alternatives & why they fail.** Willpower ("그만 봐야지"), vague to-dos, occasional digital detox. They
fail without a **replacement structure**; the evidence-backed path is **structured alternative activities + app
limits + notifications off** (multicomponent), and cutting SNS to ~30 min/day **reduced loneliness and depression**. `[S21][S20]`

**What works (evidence).** A **designed day of concrete time blocks** substitutes for the feed; time-blocking cuts
decision fatigue and gives the prefrontal cortex an anticipated structure. `[S6][S11][S21]`

**Success / failure signals.** *Success*: feed-hours converted into a few **executed blocks**; less doom-scrolling.
*Failure*: the plan exists but the day still dissolves into short-form.

**Fit with LifePlanner.** D-1 time-block design + exact-time alerts **supply the missing structure and re-cue the
action the feed made them forget** — directly countering "TikTok brain."
